FODMAPPA


I designed a food tracking app from the logo to the style guide to high-fidelity mockups.

Logo Design & Initial Style Guide

The goal of this logo was to create a logomark and logotype that would be scalable, readable, and iconic. I also wanted to make the logo food-related, specifically low-FODMAP foods. I made a few sketches to figure out a design.



I decided to try making a logo based on the carrot icon, so I made several more sketches.



I designed the final logo on Adobe Illustrator using the carrot iconography. I also created a tentative style guide to go along with the logo and potential promotional materials for the company. The final style guide was compiled in a memo for the "client."


The logo and initial style guide for FODMAPPA.

Competitor Critique

Before making the mockups of the FODMAPPA app, I researched and analyzed a similar competing app, Cara. I wrote a critique of Cara meant to both inform the client of the strengths and weaknesses of competitors and to gain knowledge of what types of UI strategies are effective for the task at hand. I downloaded the app on my phone and used it for a while to get inspiration for my UI mockups.




An in-depth UX/UI critique of competing app, Cara.

High Fidelity Mockups & Final Style Guide

Finally, after thoroughly researching my competitor's apps and designing a tentative style guide, I created 8 high-fidelity mockups for FODMAPPA. I used Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop to create UI elements and develop my final product. One of the most valuable things I learned from this exercise is using a grid system to design an application. It really helped with the alignment and balance of the interface and streamlined the design process. I will certainly be using this strategy in the future since it was incredibly efficient and time-saving.
