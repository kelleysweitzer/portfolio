SHUFFLE BUDDY


For my final project in EECS 183: Elementary Programming Concepts, I made a super fun music quiz.
Don't just take my word for it, play it for yourself!

Planning

In the planning stages of our project, our team decided to create mockups of what we wanted our final product to look like. We also agreed to implement embedded YouTube videos and come up with a way to allow users to create and play custom playlists. None of us had any experience with web development, but we chose this project out of the other options because we all wanted to use this skill in the future and were eager to learn. Incidentally, all of the team members played instruments and we shared a love of music, so making Shuffle Buddy was the obvious choice.



Our initial mockups for Shuffle Buddy

The Core

The initial part of our project involved implementing the back-end of the website and launching our website on Heroku, an application hosting site. During this phase of the project, I learned how to use Git and GitHub to collaborate with my teammates on code. I really liked being able to explain my code contributions and tracking my and my group's progress.

We used Flask and Jinja in conjunction to build a dynamic website framework upon which data could be interchanged. I really liked this method of website development because we could build a comprehensive, algorithmic back-end in Python while maintaining a flexible and responsive aesthetic on the front-end.

We utilized the iTunes API and a third-party Billboard Music Charts parser to generate the quiz questions. We used cookies to store the player's score and implemented a "clear score" function.

One of my favorite parts of this part of the project was discovering tons of new songs!
